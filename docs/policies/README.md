# Store Policies — drafts for Fazarim Cosmetics

> ⚠️ **These are starting drafts, not legal advice.**
> The client must read, adapt, and approve every policy before launch. Anything in
> `[SQUARE BRACKETS]` is a placeholder that only the client can fill.

Shopify shows these at checkout and links them in the footer. A store without a refund
policy is a genuine problem — Pakistani consumer expectations and card-network rules both
assume one exists.

## Where they go

```text
https://admin.shopify.com/store/fazarim-cosmetics/settings/policies
```

Each policy has an **"Insert template"** button — Shopify's generic starting text.
Use these drafts instead: they already reflect the store's real configuration
(Rs 250 shipping, free over Rs 5,000, Pakistan-only delivery, <ceo@fazarim.com>).

Paste each file's contents into the matching field, then **Save**.

| file | Shopify field |
|------|---------------|
| `refund-policy.md` | Refund policy |
| `shipping-policy.md` | Shipping policy |
| `privacy-policy.md` | Privacy policy |

## Before the client signs off, they must decide

1. **Returns window** — drafts say **7 days**. Common in Pakistan; some stores offer 14.
2. **Are opened cosmetics returnable?** Drafts say **no**, for hygiene reasons. This is the
   norm, and it's the single clause most likely to cause disputes. Make it unmissable.
3. **Who pays return postage?** Drafts say the customer, unless the item was faulty or wrong.
4. **Refund method** — bank transfer / Easypaisa / JazzCash? Drafts leave this open. Card
   refunds route back automatically; **Cash on Delivery refunds do not** and need a manual process.
5. **Phone** — still a placeholder. The business address is filled in (Office no. 401, Building A1, Sheranwala Heights, near Izmir Town, Canal Road, Lahore) — the Shopify *store address setting* remains blocked on the Pakistan business entity (see `LAUNCH_CHECKLIST.md`).
6. **GST / sales tax registration** — the store currently reads "Not collecting". If the client
   is registered, the Terms and pricing copy must say whether prices include tax.

## Note on the privacy policy

The draft covers what a standard Shopify store actually collects. If the client later adds
apps — analytics, marketing pixels, reviews, chat widgets — those each collect data and the
policy needs updating. Meta Pixel and Google Analytics are the usual additions.

Pakistan has no GDPR-equivalent in force today, but the store is reachable from the EU and UK.
If the client ever ships there, the policy needs a lawful-basis section. Right now Markets is
Pakistan-only, so this is deferred, not solved.
